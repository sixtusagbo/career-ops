# CronEdge original job description (Upwork, invite-only)

Title: Website Designer and Developer for Tech Startup. Posted ~3 weeks before the contract; Worldwide.

## Overview

Experienced website designer and developer to build the official website for CronEdge, a technology consulting company specializing in DevOps, cloud infrastructure, platform engineering, automation, and managed operational support.

More than a simple marketing website. They want a modern, professional platform that can grow with the business and eventually support customer onboarding, service requests, account management, and payment integrations.

Hosted in their self-managed Kubernetes environment running in their homelab infrastructure.

The solution should be:
- Containerized using Docker
- Deployable to Kubernetes
- CI/CD friendly
- Production-ready
- Easily maintainable and extensible

## Project goals

- Establish credibility and trust with prospective clients
- Clearly communicate services and expertise
- Generate leads and consultation requests
- Support future customer self-service capabilities
- Designed with scalability and maintainability in mind

## Initial website pages

Home, About Us, Services, Industries We Serve, Pricing, Careers, Contact Us, Blog / Resources.

## Technical requirements

Preferred: Next.js, React, TypeScript, Tailwind CSS, responsive design, SEO optimized, fast page load, experience with Kubernetes-hosted applications preferred.

Bonus: experience designing SaaS or consulting company websites; experience building customer portals; experience with Stripe integrations; experience with ticketing/helpdesk systems.

## Deliverables

- Complete website design
- Responsive frontend implementation
- Source code
- Deployment documentation
- Recommendations for future portal architecture

## They asked applicants to provide

Portfolio of similar projects, relevant experience, recommended technology stack, estimated timeline, estimated project cost.

## Notes for the portal conversation

The portal vision is already defined here, not something to discover from scratch: customer onboarding, service requests, account management, payment integrations (Stripe), and ticketing/helpdesk (bonus). The "Recommendations for future portal architecture" deliverable was built as docs/PORTAL-ARCHITECTURE.md in the site repo, lead with that on the portal topic.
